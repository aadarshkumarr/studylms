Responsibilities of Data Processor

A data processor is responsible for encoding various information to the organization's database, originating from either manual or electronic communications. They must be highly detail-oriented, especially on analyzing the completeness of data before uploading it to the system.

Data processors help companies more efficiently predict and prioritize outcomes, provide responses to user inquiries, and define, improve, and nurture relationships with customers.

Joint controllers must enter into an arrangement setting out their respective responsibilities for complying with the GDPR rules, and the data processor processes personal data only on behalf of the controller.

Here are the responsibilities of a data processor:

  • Follow instructions: Process personal data only in accordance with the documented instructions provided by the data controller. Do not use or process the data for any other purposes without explicit authorization.
  • Implement security measures: Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect the personal data you process. This includes encryption, access controls, regular security updates, and employee training on data protection best practices.
  • Maintain confidentiality: Maintain the confidentiality of the personal data you process. Ensure that only authorized personnel have access to the data and that they are bound by strict confidentiality obligations.
  • Cooperate with data controllers: Cooperate with the data controller to fulfil their obligations under data protection laws. This includes assisting with data subject rights requests, data breach notifications, and privacy impact assessments, as required.
  • Maintain records: Maintain accurate records of your processing activities, including the types of personal data processed, the purposes of processing, and any transfers of data to third parties. This will help demonstrate compliance with data protection regulations.

Here are some things that you should not do as a data processor:

  • Do not process data without authorization: Do not process personal data beyond the scope of the instructions provided by the data controller. Obtain explicit authorization if there is a need to deviate from the documented instructions.
  • Do not retain data indefinitely: Do not retain personal data for longer than necessary. Process data only for the period specified by the data controller or as required by applicable laws or regulations.
  • Do not use data for unauthorized purposes: Do not use personal data for any purposes other than those explicitly authorized by the data controller. Obtain additional consent if you wish to use the data for other purposes.
  • Do not share data without authorization: Do not share personal data with third parties unless explicitly authorized by the data controller or required by law. If sharing is necessary, ensure that appropriate data protection agreements are in place with third parties.
  • Do not neglect security measures: Neglecting to implement appropriate security measures can expose personal data to unauthorized access or breaches. Prioritize data security and regularly update your systems and safeguards.
  • Do not ignore data breaches: In the event of a data breach, do not delay or ignore your responsibility to promptly notify the data controller and respond appropriately to mitigate the breach and prevent future incidents.
