Data Protection Table

S No. Particulars Do's Don'ts
1 Data Limitation Personal data should only be collected for the purposes for which it is required Personal data should not be used for a different purpose or stored indefinitely just because an organization believes it may be useful in the future
2 Data Minimization When the purpose for which the Organization retains personal data has expired, ensure that all records are securely deleted or destroyed, preferably by shredding paper documents and disposing of them in a confidential waste bin Do not keep inaccurate data as this is a breach of data protection legislation as applicable
3 Accuracy At least once a year, review the information you have on file for any individual. This will ensure that the Organization's records are accurate and up to date. Personal data should not be stored or sent on removable media such as a USB (Universal Serial Bus) pen drive as it may be easily lost or stolen
4 Data Portability Once an electronic device has reached the end of its useful life, ensure that all data/information is erased, and the hard drive is wiped. The device should be disposed of securely Do not encourage the use of personal devices for business purposes. Wherever possible, issue phones, laptops, and other items to individuals for official business and ensure that they are returned at the end of that person's role
5 Right to access Always remember that a data subject has the right to inspect the information/data that an organization has on file for them. Organizations must exercise caution when storing information and ensure that it can be retrieved quickly Do not make any comments about individuals that the Organization will be unable to defend if challenged. Individual opinions are classified as personal data, and organizations should assume that the data subject will read everything
6 Right of Data Subject Organizations should ensure that personal data is stored in such a way that it can be accessed quickly if an individual makes "Data Subject Access Request" to access their personal information Do not change or delete personal data that you know is subject to a "Data Subject Access Request" from an individual seeking access to their personal information
7 Password Protection All computers, screensavers, and documents should be password protected securely Passwords should not be written down and should be changed frequently.
8 Encrypt Files All electronic communications containing personal data, particularly sensitive (referred to as "special category") personal data, should always be encrypted If possible, avoid sending confidential communications via email; at the very least, such communications should be encrypted
9 Security Measures The organization should ensure that all data protection policies and procedures are understood by all employees. Keep a record so that the organization can demonstrate that this requirement was met Ensure that all visitors are escorted out of the office/building to prevent unauthorized access
Never open emails from unknown senders. If the email appears suspicious, call the sender before reading it or opening any attachments
10 Data Breach Record Regularly inspect and keep track of any data breaches. For information on what constitutes a data breach, how to prevent one, and what to do if data breaches occur Except as specified in the Privacy Notice, do not routinely disclose personal information to third parties without their consent. For a better understanding of the concept of consent and the time constraints when consent is required. Always ensure Lawful grounds of PII (Personally Identifiable Information) data processing
11 Consent Before processing personal data, prior consent must be obtained Do not assume that a data subject's consent is permanent. They have the right to withdraw their consent for data processing at any time
