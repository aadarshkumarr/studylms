Responsibilities of Data Controller

The responsibilities of a data controller include determining the purpose and manner in which personal data is processed, ensuring compliance with the Data Protection Regulations, establishing a legal precedent for collecting data, and creating a privacy policy that outlines the purpose of data collection and the entities with whom the data is shared.

The data processor, on the other hand, is responsible for processing data on behalf of the data controller and cannot be employed by the data controller. Data processors tend to be third-party services.

Here are the responsibilities of a data controller:

  • Obtain informed consent: This means getting permission from individuals before collecting their personal data. You must explain why you need the data and how you will use it.
  • Have a lawful basis for processing: This means that you must have a legal reason to collect and use personal data. For example, you may need to collect data to provide a service to the individual, or to comply with a legal requirement.
  • Limit data collection and processing: Only collect the data that you need for your specific purpose. Do not collect excessive or unnecessary data.
  • Implement security measures: Protect personal data from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure. This includes using encryption, access controls, and other security measures.
  • Be transparent: Be clear about your data collection practices. Let individuals know what data you collect, how you use it, and who you share it with.
  • Respect individual rights: Individuals have the right to access, correct, delete, or restrict the use of their personal data. You must respect these rights and respond to requests promptly.
  • Safely store and retain data: Keep personal data for as long as you need it. Once you no longer need it, securely dispose of it.
  • Establish data protection policies: Develop and implement policies and procedures to protect personal data. Train employees on these policies to ensure compliance.

Here are some things that you should not do as a data controller:

  • Do not collect unnecessary data: Do not collect personal data that is not relevant or necessary for your intended purpose.
  • Do not share data without consent: Do not share personal data with third parties without the individual's consent.
  • Do not use data for unauthorized purposes: Do not use personal data for purposes that are beyond the scope of the individual's consent or without a legitimate legal basis.
  • Do not neglect security measures: Do not neglect to implement adequate security measures to protect personal data from unauthorized access or breaches.
  • Do not retain data indefinitely: Do not retain personal data for longer than necessary. Establish clear retention periods and securely dispose of data when it is no longer needed.
  • Do not ignore privacy policies: Do not neglect to have comprehensive and transparent privacy policies in place. Develop and maintain up-to-date privacy policies that inform individuals about your data collection practices
  • Do not mislead individuals: Do not mislead individuals about your data collection practices, purpose, or how their data will be used. Be transparent and provide accurate information to ensure informed consent.
